A Mole of Rubik's Cubes

by Ayaan Raza


Information:

Dimenisons A standard 3×3 Rubik’s Cube measures about 57 mm on each side, meaning that each individual cubie is approximately 19 mm in dimension




Avogadro's Number: 6.022×1023

How far would it go
  • Cube Edge Length: 57 mm (0.057 m)
  • Number of Cubes: 6.022×1023
  • Total Length in Meters: 6.022×1023 × 0.057 ≈ 3.43×1022 m
  • Total Length in Kilometers: 3.43×1022 m ÷ 1,000 ≈ 3.43×1019 km
  • Total Length in Light Years: 3.43×1022 m ÷ 9.461×1015 (m/ly) ≈ 3.63×106 ly


Imagine aligning an Avogadro’s number (6.022×1023) of 57 mm cubes end-to-end. This chain would span roughly 3.43×1022 meters, or about 3.63 million light years in length. For context, the Moon is just about 384,400 km away and the Sun around 150 million km - distances that are negligible compared to this cosmic scale. Even the nearest star, Proxima Centauri, lies only about 4.24 light years from Earth, and our Milky Way galaxy itself spans roughly 100,000 light years. In contrast, this chain would stretch well beyond the distance to the Andromeda Galaxy (approximately 2.5 million light years away) and would be over 36 times the diameter of our galaxy, showing the magnitude of Avogadro's number.
